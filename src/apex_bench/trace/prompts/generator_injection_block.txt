## Strategy cheatsheet from prior cases in this area

Below is a cheatsheet of ATOMIC STRATEGY BULLETS curated from prior
cases on structurally similar problems. Each bullet captures a
procedure, framework, convention, definitional pitfall, or output-
format requirement worth knowing for this kind of case. Bullets carry
usage counters: `helpful` is how often the bullet was cited on a
case that was judged correct, `harmful` is how often it was cited on
an incorrect case, `usage` is the total cite count.

You may consult any bullet that applies. Do NOT copy specific
numerical values, named entities, file paths, or named references
from a bullet — values in bullets are illustrative; the current case
has different actual ones. Use each bullet as a methodology to
execute on the current case's actual data.

{bullets_block}

When you have completed the deliverable, on the LAST line of your
response, list the `bullet_id` of every bullet you actually consulted
during the work, in this exact format:

    <citations>[bullet-1, bullet-7]</citations>

If you consulted no bullets, write:

    <citations>[]</citations>

The citations line is internal bookkeeping; it is stripped from the
deliverable before the rubric is applied.
